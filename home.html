Salam Redaksi

A journey is more meaningful when every stop changes how we see the city.

Jejak Jayakarta is not simply a list of places to visit. It is a sequence of experiences designed to move from observation to understanding, and then from understanding to contribution.

Museum MACAN introduces new ways of seeing. Museum Kota Tua asks us to examine the layers of Jakarta’s history. Tzu Chi turns environmental concern into practical action. SAAJA closes the journey through direct learning and service with children.

— The Jejak Jayakarta editorial team

The four-day route

Each visit happens on a different day so the group has enough time to observe, participate, discuss, and reflect.

  1. 01
    4 hours Contemporary art

    Museum MACAN

    Gallery orientation, guided observation, selected exhibit exploration, group interpretation, and a short reflection.

    ObserveInterpretDiscuss
  2. 02
    4.5 hours Urban history

    Museum Kota Tua

    Historic district walk, museum visit, architectural observation, source comparison, and discussion about whose stories are represented.

    TraceCompareQuestion
  3. 03
    3.5 hours Environmental action

    Tzu Chi: Sort Plastic

    Waste-system introduction, sorting demonstration, supervised activity, discussion of contamination, and a personal habit commitment.

    SortActCommit
  4. 04
    5 hours Community learning

    SAAJA: Teach Kids

    Team briefing, learning activities, small-group facilitation, breaks, feedback, cleanup, and a final reflection on service and reciprocity.

    PrepareTeachListen
